About me

After earning my B.S. in Informatics from SUNY Albany (with a focus on UX/UI), I spent time building OpenTI, a creative studio where I worked with small brands to craft websites and brand identities. That experience taught me how to take ideas from concept to launch — and how to design with both creativity and clarity.

Since then, I’ve been focused on my startup, TchPack — a platform that helps fashion designers bring their ideas to life by using AI to match them with trusted manufacturers. Leading design and development there has deepened my skills in product thinking, UI/UX design, and front-end execution — all while building for real users and tight timelines.

This portfolio is a snapshot of my journey as a designer and developer.

What I've been working on

Right now, I’m focused full-time on building Tchpack — a platform that helps clothing designers and creators make better products. We quickly grew it into a full product ecosystem:an Adobe Illustrator plugin that generates mockups from images, a mobile app that connects users with manufacturers, generates mockups, and a marketplace for sourcing blank garments.

We've been designing, building, and marketing every piece of it — from UI and product flows to user acquisition experiments. The goal is to make it easier for creators to go from idea to finished product without needing a whole team behind them.

I am currently also full-time for ITS, and I’ve worked on several personal projects exploring UX, storytelling, and digital spaces. My background in interactive design (and a bit of game dev) has shaped how I think about creative tools and user experience.

Design should be simple

My background in design might not be the most extensive, but since I can remember, I've always loved working with tech & design.

I enjoy experimenting with new tech like VR headsets, Lego engineering kits, and the newest gaming consoles. I've also had a passion for art/design for majority of my life. One of my main missions in life is about making design and technology accessible for everyone and adaptive to each person.

I believe good design should be simple and clear with a pragmatic focus on business growth.
