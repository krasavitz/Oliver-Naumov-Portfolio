Trace

SUNY Albany CINF 302

Fall 2023

Context

I took SUNY Albany's CINF 302 course on User Interface Design. For this class, I created a digital solution for a real or conceptual company. I developed "Trace," a startup focused on personal digital privacy management. This project gave me the opportunity to apply rapid prototyping techniques and visual design principles in a real-world context while simulating the experience of working with a client.

Problem

Trace found a big gap in the privacy management market. While there are lots of companies offering individual privacy solutions, no one had come up with a viable platform for managing your entire digital footprint. My challenge was to design an intuitive app that helps users take back control of their personal information that's scattered all over the internet.

Role

For this concept project, I was responsible for the UX/UI design. This included creating the brand identity for Trace, developing user personas and journey maps, designing the visual system and style guide, creating wireframes and prototypes, and applying rapid prototyping methodologies learned in class.

Impact

Trace was a conceptual project that let me apply UX/UI design principles in a practical setting. I designed a brand and product that worked together well, and the project showed that I understand UX/UI design principles. Trace could be the basis for future development.
